Exhibit A: Ripple Neuro

Objectives:

Objective #1: Redesign outdated website and implement a cohesive marketing strategy to create a modern communications funnel.

Objective #2: Highlight new products while increasing industry brand awareness.

Objective #3: Generate warm leads so that sales will have better conversations with prospects.


Target Audience:

Academic researchers (primarily neuroscientists) with grant money to spare.

Their primary driver in choosing a system is based on arguably two concerns:
"can it accomplish what I need to do right now", and "how adaptable is it for
future experiments?"


Audience Messaging:

I decided to highlight Ripple's unique value offerings: extremely customizable systems, and delightful customer service representatives who help scientists navigate the complex process of integrating new technology with their existing workflow.


Competitive Insight:

Their competitors rarely pay much attention to direct marketing, and instead rely on word of mouth or trade show attendance. Most of their websites are outdated, and very few have a coherent communications strategy.

Presenting a clean, user-focused website that highlights what customers can do with their systems would immediately set Ripple ahead of its competition.

Also, since trade shows play such an integral role in the sales process of these devices,
I knew a significant part of the marketing budget should be dedicated to creating pre/post-show email marketing & quality print materials to distribute in person.


Plan:

Craft effective messaging and brand guidelines to leverage across channels.

Hire a web design firm to develop a product-centric web presence.

Develop email marketing timeline and strategies

Go all out on trade show marketing. Host parties. Give away prizes in exchange for contact information.

Analyze web traffic before, during, and after marketing pushes.

Remain in constant contact with sales teams to ensure customer-centric marketing strategies align with their insight from the field.


Execution:


Style Guide



Event Planning

Party Invite (business card size)



Banner stands (6 ft.) for trade show booth


Brochure Design

Abbreviated brochure



Full length product brochure (sample pages)



Website


Link



Results:

Doubled web traffic within first few months.

Curated attendence at events (e.g. Society for Neuroscience) brought in increased numbers of quality sales leads

Hosted successful marketing parties that roused significant interest amongst the scientific community, and built personal relationships with prospects.

Sales reported a 15% increase in inbound leads, pointing to the website redesign, as they now have an array of informative places where prospects can learn about each product offering.
