Exhibit A: Sales Development eBook

Overview:

Great content is important for any organization, but for SaaS-based sales business intelligence solutions - it's absolutely essential.

That's because the people who purchase these solutions (sales leaders) are
a specific type of individual.

They're confident, outgoing, and insist on data-driven, results-oriented decision making in every aspect of their professional life.

Datanyze commissioned me to interview successful sales representatives, and then write an eBook (along with several blog posts) to spread their knowledge.

Doing so would continue to bolster their brand as a thought leader in their vertical (business intelligence solutions for SaaS companies).

Ideally, it would also function as onboarding material for new biz dev hires throughout the SaaS community.


Target Audience:

Sales managers & oncoming sales hires.


Audience Messaging:

Modern SaaS sales teams speak to one another in a hyper-direct tone that's concise, realistic, and targets the data-driven, results-oriented world they live in.


Competitive Insight:

The business intelligence SaaS solution world is a crowded marketplace, with numerous companies creating remarkably similar products and relevant content.

Real-world knowledge that stems directly from sales professionals is the only type of writing that is respected amongst target audiences.

Ensure interview deadlines are met.

Approve design and publish.

Exhibit B: Ghostwritten Blog Posts

Overview:

I worked with Datanyze sales executives to write journalism-centric blog posts, to be published under their names.


Target Audience:

Sales managers and SDRs


Audience Messaging:

Writing tone: straightforward, casual, concise, data-driven.


Plan:

Pitch content ideas and narrow down the best topics.

Interview sales leaders and take great notes.

Revise: Draft a first go to receive honest feedback.

Go through this revision process 1-3 more times, as needed.

Publish and track metrics to learn what sorts of topics resonate best.


Execution:

"How To Prevent Your SDRs from Burning Out"



Link


Results:

This became one of their top pieces of content, in terms of shares and comments.
